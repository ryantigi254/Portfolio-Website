The Challenge

Neurodivergent children, particularly those with autism spectrum disorder (ASD) and sensory processing disorders (SPD), experience significant distress due to sensory dysregulation. This distress presents as sensory hyper-responsivity, hypo-responsivity, or sensory-seeking behaviors, leading to challenges in:

  • Social interaction and communication
  • Emotional regulation and anxiety management
  • Academic performance and learning
  • Daily functioning and independence

Traditional interventions like occupational therapy or static sensory tools (e.g., noise-cancelling headphones) fail to address these sensory triggers in dynamic, real-world environments, creating a critical gap in support for these children and their families.

Our Vision

SensoryNeural represents our vision for an adaptive environment that supports neurodivergent children's sensory needs. By combining artificial intelligence with everyday surroundings, we aim to create spaces that adjust to the child rather than requiring the child to adjust to rigid environments.

Our approach shifts from reactive interventions to proactive support, leveraging emerging technologies to predict and address sensory challenges before they lead to distress. The goal is to empower neurodivergent children with greater autonomy, inclusion, and overall well-being.

Our ultimate vision is to shape an inclusive future where neurodivergent children have the same opportunities to thrive as their neurotypical peers.

Conceptual Features

Predictive Sensory Support

Anticipating potential sensory triggers before they cause distress, allowing for timely interventions.

Environmental Adaptations

Creating sensory-friendly spaces that can adjust in real-time to meet individual needs.

Personalized Approach

Recognizing that each child has unique sensory preferences and needs that evolve over time.

Multi-Environment Support

Extending sensory accommodations beyond the home to educational and community settings.

Data-Informed Insights

Providing parents and professionals with meaningful information to better understand and support the child.

Promoting Independence

Helping children develop self-regulation skills while providing necessary environmental support.

Impact Potential

We believe SensoryNeural has the potential to transform daily life for:

  • Children with sensory processing challenges: Increasing comfort, reducing anxiety, and improving engagement in educational and social activities
  • Families: Reducing stress and providing tools to better support their children's unique needs
  • Educators: Enabling more inclusive classroom environments that accommodate diverse sensory profiles
  • Healthcare providers: Offering new insights and approaches to supporting children with sensory regulation challenges

By addressing sensory needs proactively, we aim to help remove barriers to participation and learning, ultimately contributing to better outcomes for neurodivergent children.
